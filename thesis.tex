\section{History of the Web}
To better understand what the Web 3.0 is and what the term stands for, we can analyse the history of the two previous iterations of the web.
First of, it is important to understand, that the evolution of the web 1.0 to the web 2.0 didn't take place at a concrete time. It was rather a slow progress, where certain websites gradually implemented additional functionalities and technologies, eg. javascript, AJAX. \\



\textbf{Web 1.0}\\

The first "packet-switched" network, the ARPANET (Advanced Research Project Agency Network), was created in 1969 in the United States. This network connected four Universities and is seen as one of the basic building blocks of the web.
The "world-wide web" or web for short, has been established by Tim Burners-Lee in late 1989.
